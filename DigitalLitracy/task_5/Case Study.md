### Cyber-Ambassador Resource: Phishing Awareness





#### Overview of the Threat

Phishing is a deceptive cybercrime where malicious actors masquerade as reputable entities—such as university administration, major banks, or popular e-commerce sites—to manipulate individuals into surrendering sensitive data. This often includes login credentials, credit card numbers, or Government ID details.



#### The Anatomy of an Attack



&#x20;   **The Hook:** You receive a message (email, SMS, or WhatsApp) that creates a high-pressure situation, such as claiming your college registration is about to be canceled or a "limited-time" internship offer is expiring.



&#x20;   **The Redirection:** The message contains a link that directs you to a fraudulent portal. These sites are meticulously designed to mimic the official VITyarthi or banking login pages.



&#x20;   **The Harvest**: Once you enter your details, the attacker captures them in real-time. In some variations, simply clicking the link triggers a background download of spyware onto your device.



##### Potential Impact

The fallout from a successful phishing attempt can be devastating, ranging from total identity theft and unauthorized financial withdrawals to the compromise of academic records and professional reputations.
