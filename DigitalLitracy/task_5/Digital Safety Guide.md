### Digital Safety: Student Prevention Guide





#### Core Defense Strategies



&#x20;   Unique Credentials: Implement a strategy of using distinct, complex passwords for every academic and personal account to prevent "credential stuffing" attacks.



&#x20;   Hardened Authentication: Mandatory use of Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), preferably via an authenticator app rather than SMS, adds a critical second layer of defense.



&#x20;   Link Skepticism: Hover over any link before clicking to inspect the actual destination URL; if the domain looks slightly "off" (e.g., .net instead of .ac.in), do not click.



&#x20;   Software Hygiene: Ensure your operating system and browser are set to auto-update to patch vulnerabilities that phishers often exploit.

#### Financial \& UPI Security Protocol



&#x20;   The PIN Rule: Never input your UPI PIN when receiving money. A PIN is strictly for authorizing outgoing payments.



&#x20;   Request Verification: Before approving any "Collect" request on apps like PhonePe or GPay, verify the sender’s identity through a separate communication channel.



&#x20;   QR Code Caution: Avoid scanning random QR codes in public spaces or those sent by unknown individuals claiming you have won a prize.



&#x20;   No Screen Sharing: Never engage in screen-sharing apps (like AnyDesk or TeamViewer) while troubleshooting payment issues with supposed "customer care."
