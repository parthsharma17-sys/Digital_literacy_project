## Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students



##### The Do's (Responsible Use)



&#x20;   Privacy Audit: Regularly check your account privacy settings to control who can see your personal posts and tagged photos.



&#x20;   Professional Branding: Use platforms like LinkedIn to highlight your academic achievements, skills, and projects.



&#x20;   Fact-Check Content: Verify the authenticity of news or information before sharing it to prevent the spread of misinformation.



&#x20;   Positive Engagement: Use your digital presence to support peers, share educational resources, and network with industry professionals.



&#x20;   Secure Your Accounts: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) and use strong, unique passwords for every social platform.



##### The Don'ts (Risks to Avoid)



&#x20;   Over-Sharing: Avoid posting sensitive personal information such as your home address, current real-time location, or financial details.



&#x20;   Negative Footprint: Never post or engage in content that involves cyberbullying, hate speech, or offensive language, as this can be tracked by future employers.



&#x20;   Unverified Links: Do not click on suspicious links or "viral" quizzes that request access to your social media profile data.



&#x20;   Plagiarism: Avoid sharing someone else’s creative work or research without giving proper credit or obtaining permission.



&#x20;   Public Venting: Refrain from posting unprofessional complaints about professors, peers, or your institution, as digital archives are often permanent.
